Schrodinger bridge

What is a Schrödinger Bridge?

The Schrödinger Bridge (SB) problem is a classical problem appearing in applied mathematics, optimal control and probability; see [1, 2, 3]. In the discrete-time setting, it takes the following (dynamic) form. Consider as reference density $p(x_{0:N})$ describing the process adding noise to the data. We aim to find $p^*(x_{0:N})$ such that $p^*(x_0) = p_{\mathrm{data}}(x_0)$ and $p^*(x_N) = p_{\mathrm{prior}}(x_N)$ and minimize the Kullback-Leibler divergence between $p^*$ and $p$. In this work we introduce Diffusion Schrodinger Bridge (DSB), a new algorithm which uses score-matching approaches [4] to approximate the Iterative Proportional Fitting algorithm, an iterative method to find the solutions of the SB problem. DSB can be seen as a refinement of existing score-based generative modeling methods [5, 6].
